About me

My name is Merel and I am 20 years old.

Design has interested me since I was

a little girl. Currently I am studying

'Engineering Product Design' and just

finished my second year. For me, the key

of this study, is to design produts that

are both functional and beautiful. I think

it's important to focus on the appearence

of products, to create value for a brand

and to make products more attractive for

customers and users. My open and social

character helps me working in teams that

are formed for each new assignment, and

also working on my own project I find energizing.

Lamp

The aim of this project was to design a lamp that did not require external

connection components. The lamp had to be assembled by self-designed

connections. Part of the requirements was to use only one type of material;

a sheet of polyethylene. We had to use a laser cutter to make the lamp.

This lamp is called 'the surprise' because

when the light is off it looks like a normal

lamp but as soon as the light goes on you will

see many patterns appear.

Design and production process

It was a design process of a few weeks in which I

first looked up inspiration and then started

making sketches on paper. In the next step I

designed the lamp using Adobe illustrator and

had it laser cutted a couple of times. With every

print I corrected errors and improved the design

which led to the final product

Design for Space

Interior design for outside. The Amsterdam

Arenaboulevard is a large empty space, for this

assignment we had to come up with a way to fill

up the space and make it feel more intimate.

After a process of a couple of weeks I came

up with the idea of a promenade.

I started with interviews of visitors of the boulevard and

personnel of the stores in the area. I synthesised their

opinions with my own ideas of what would be required

and came up with the concept of a promenade. I've made

different moodboards and ended up with the version

shown here that I used for the design.

Package Design

In this assignment you received a brand, a product and a

type of packaging. I had to design a flexible package that

can hold soft drinks for the brand 'the city of Amsterdam'.

After looking up inspiration from the brand of the city of Amsterdam and

examples of flexible liquid containers, I starded of making sketches. Keeping

ergonomics in mind I combined different ideas and modelled it in Solidworks.

The grip of the bottle is comfortable and sturdy and the big cap makes it

easy to open and reclose.
